REAL MADRID'S HISTORY

Three years after its founding, ie in 1905, the team named "Madrid FC" was able to win the first official local championship,
after defeating Athletic Bilbao in the Spanish Cup final . The team managed to retain the championship title for four consecutive years
(it was the only official championship held at that time).
The club was one of the founding members of the Spanish Football Federation on January 4, 1909 , when the team was represented by its president, Adolfo Melendez ,
to sign the agreement to establish the Spanish Federation. In 1912, the team moved to its good stadium,
which is called "Campo de O'Donnell" after the team was moving from one place to another due to the lack of a private stadium.
In 1920, the name of the team was changed to Real Madrid by King Alfonso XIII of Spain, who was given the title "Real", which means "royal" to the team.
